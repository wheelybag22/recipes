Chicken Fajita Seasoning

Chicken

A bold, smoky blend for fajitas — enough to season about 1.5 lbs of chicken. Scale up and store in an airtight jar for easy weeknight cooking.

Ingredients
IngredientAmount (4 servings)
Chili powder2 tsp
Cumin1½ tsp
Smoked paprika1 tsp
Garlic powder1 tsp
Onion powder½ tsp
Dried oregano½ tsp
Salt1 tsp
Black pepper½ tsp
Cayenne pepper¼ tsp
Instructions
  1. Mix all spices together in a small bowl until evenly blended.
  2. Pat chicken dry, then coat all sides generously with the seasoning mix. Let sit for 15–30 minutes before cooking.
  3. Cook in a very hot cast iron or skillet with a little oil over high heat, about 5–6 minutes per side, until nicely charred and cooked through. Rest 5 minutes before slicing.
Notes
  • Heat level: Adjust cayenne up or down to taste. Double it for a real kick.
  • Big batch: Scale up and store in an airtight jar — keeps 6 months. Use about 1 tbsp per pound of chicken.
  • Marinade option: Mix seasoning with 2 tbsp olive oil and juice of 1 lime. Marinate chicken 1–4 hours for deeper flavor.

Egg Roll In A Bowl

Beef

A hearty, flavor-packed skillet dish with all the taste of egg rolls — no wrapper needed. Great for weeknights.

Ingredients
IngredientAmount
Lean ground beef1.25–2 lbs
Fine shredded cabbage10 oz bag
Red shredded cabbage10 oz bag
Green onions1 bunch
Garlic cloves4–8 cloves
Minced ginger2 tbsp
Red bell pepper1
Gochujang1 tbsp
Hoisin sauce3 tbsp
Soy sauce¼ cup
Toasted sesame oil2 tsp
Rice vinegar2 tbsp
Directions
  1. Brown the ground beef in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. While the beef cooks, thinly slice the red bell pepper, chop the green onions, and mince the garlic and ginger.
  3. Drain the beef, return it to the pan, and add toasted sesame oil, rice vinegar, hoisin, gochujang, and soy sauce. Cook for 2–3 minutes.
  4. Add both cabbages, bell pepper, green onions, garlic, and ginger. Stir to combine and cook until the veggies are tender and reduced, about 15–20 minutes.

Tomato Bisque

Soup

A rich, roasted tomato bisque with sweet potato and Gruyère — deeply flavorful and silky smooth.

Ingredients
IngredientAmount
Roma tomatoes6–8
Sugar bomb cherry tomatoes1 pint
Small sweet potato, peeled & cubed1
Red bell pepper1
Gruyère cheeseto taste
Fresh garlic cloves6–8 cloves
Fresh thyme (or dried)3–4 stalks (or 1 tbsp)
Heavy cream8 oz
Chicken broth16 oz
Olive oilfor drizzling
Directions
  1. Chop Roma tomatoes and peeled sweet potato into cubes. Chop red bell pepper into chunks. Peel garlic and leave sugar bomb tomatoes whole. Arrange on a sheet pan, drizzle with olive oil, and roast at 425°F for 30–45 minutes.
  2. Transfer all roasted veggies and all pan juices to a large pot.
  3. Add half the chicken broth and blend with an immersion blender until smooth. Add the remaining broth once it's less chunky. Simmer on low heat for 5–10 minutes.
  4. Warm the heavy cream in the microwave so it's not cold, then stir into the pot over low heat. Blend again to incorporate. Add Gruyère and stir until fully melted and combined.

Sweet Bourbon Mushrooms

Side

The ultimate sweet and umami pairing for steak, pork, or just about anything. Caramelized golden brown in under 30 minutes.

Ingredients
IngredientAmount
Unsalted butter1 tbsp (14g)
Mushrooms, sliced2 cups (192g)
Bourbon3 tbsp (45ml)
Balsamic vinegar1 tbsp (15ml)
Soy sauce2 tsp
Garlic powder½ tsp
Sea salt½ tsp
Freshly ground black pepper½ tsp
Directions
  1. Preheat a cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat (350°F / 180°C). Add the butter 2 minutes before cooking.
  2. Add the mushrooms and cook for 5–7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they release their liquid and it has fully evaporated and the mushrooms are golden brown.
  3. Add all remaining ingredients and cook for 3–4 minutes until the liquids have reduced and thickened. Pull the skillet off the heat and serve.

New England Clam Chowder

Soup

A rich, creamy clam chowder built on a classic bacon-and-roux base with red potatoes and clams.

Ingredients
IngredientAmount
Bacon4 oz
Butter or margarine8 oz
Yellow onion, chopped12 oz
High-gluten flour12 oz
Hot water3 qts
Clam base5 oz
Red potatoes48 oz
Chopped clams (canned)1 can
Heavy cream2 qts
Directions
  1. In a large stockpot, render the bacon until crispy.
  2. Add butter and onion. Cook for 5 minutes until softened.
  3. Add flour and stir to form a roux.
  4. Temper the heavy cream before adding it to the pot.
  5. Add remaining ingredients and bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until potatoes are al dente.

Chicken Salad

Chicken

A classic chicken salad with toasted pecans, red grapes, and a honey poppy seed dressing. Serve in a sandwich, wrap, or on a lettuce bed.

Ingredients
IngredientAmount
Chicken breast32 oz
Pecans, toasted6 oz
Seedless red grapes16 oz (2 cups)
Celery stalks, sliced3 stalks
Mayonnaise8 oz
Apple cider vinegar4 tsp
Honey5 tsp
Poppy seeds2 tsp
Saltto taste
Black pepperto taste
Directions
  1. Make the dressing: whisk together mayo, vinegar, honey, poppy seeds, salt, and pepper. Refrigerate — can be made up to 2 days ahead.
  2. Preheat oven to 350°F. Place chicken in a baking dish with ½ cup water, cover with foil, and bake 25 minutes until cooked through.
  3. Remove chicken, cool slightly at room temperature, then chill completely in the refrigerator covered with plastic wrap.
  4. Dice cold chicken into bite-size pieces and transfer to a large bowl. Combine with pecans, grapes, celery, and dressing.
  5. Serve in a sandwich, wrap, or on a lettuce bed.

Garlic Parm Chicken Skewers

Chicken

Buffalo-marinated chicken skewers grilled and basted with a homemade garlic parmesan sauce. Marinate overnight for best results.

Ingredients
IngredientAmount
Large chicken breasts4–6
Buffalo sauceat least 1 cup
Butter2 tbsp
Head of garlic1
Mayonnaise½ cup
Grated Parmesan cheese2–4 oz
Apple cider vinegar1 tbsp
Lemon juice1 tsp
Salt½ tsp
Red pepper flakes¼ tsp
Dried oregano¼ tsp
Dried basil¼ tsp
Black pepper¼ tsp
Night Before
  1. Tenderize chicken breasts, then slice into thin strips cutting with the grain.
  2. Marinate in buffalo sauce overnight, or at least 4 hours.
Day Of
  1. Weave chicken strips onto skewers.
  2. Mince the garlic. Melt butter in a saucepan and cook garlic for 1–2 minutes.
  3. Combine cooked garlic with mayonnaise, Parmesan, vinegar, lemon juice, salt, red pepper flakes, oregano, basil, and black pepper. Whisk together and refrigerate until ready to use.
  4. Grill skewers, rotating every 3–4 minutes, until cooked through — about 18–20 minutes total.
  5. Baste garlic parm sauce onto chicken during the last few minutes of grilling.

Buffalo Sauce

Sauce

Classic homemade buffalo sauce built on Frank's RedHot and butter. Ready in minutes and stores for up to 2 weeks.

Ingredients
IngredientAmount
Frank's RedHot (or hot sauce of choice)½ cup
Unsalted butter¼ cup (4 tbsp)
White vinegar1 tsp
Worcestershire sauce½ tsp
Garlic powder¼ tsp
Cayenne pepper (optional)pinch
Saltto taste
Directions
  1. Melt the butter in a small saucepan over low heat, or microwave it.
  2. Whisk in the hot sauce, vinegar, Worcestershire, garlic powder, and cayenne.
  3. Taste and adjust — more hot sauce for heat, more butter for a milder, richer sauce.
  4. Use immediately or store in the fridge for up to 2 weeks. Reheat gently before using.
Tips & Variations
  • Ratio is key: Classic is roughly 2:1 hot sauce to butter. Go 1:1 for milder/richer, or 3:1 for spicier.
  • Don't boil it — just warm enough to melt the butter and combine. Boiling can break the emulsion and make it greasy.
  • Honey buffalo: Add 1–2 tbsp honey for a sweet heat version — great for wings or grilling.
  • Garlic buffalo: Sauté a clove or two of minced garlic in the butter first before adding the rest.
